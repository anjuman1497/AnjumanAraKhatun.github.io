About

Professional Profile

I am a materials scientist and R&D engineer with a Doctor of Philosophy in Energy Materials Science from the University of Yamanashi, Japan (2024), supported by the prestigious Japanese Government MEXT Scholarship. My research spans ceramic materials, crystal science, biodegradable polymers, and computational materials research using Density Functional Theory (DFT).


Currently serving as an R&D Engineer at Nakaya Co. Ltd in Yamanashi, Japan, I bring extensive experience in advanced characterization techniques, materials synthesis, and scientific publication. My work bridges fundamental materials science with applied engineering, including electron beam welding and photocatalytic materials. I aspire to contribute to impactful academic and industrial research addressing global challenges.

  • Biomedical Engineering, Polymer Materials, and Biodegradable Polymers
  • Density Functional Theory (DFT) calculations and computational materials research
  • Synthesis, processing, and characterization of ceramic materials, metal alloys, and composites
  • Advanced joining technologies — Electron Beam Welding and Laser Welding
  • Scientific writing, peer-reviewed publication, and research dissemination
  • Student supervision, academic mentoring, and teaching support
  • Research proposal development and grant writing
  • Experimental design, data analysis, and engineering problem-solving
  • Proficient in characterization instruments and scientific software for materials analysis
  • Strong leadership, teamwork, critical thinking, and communication skills
